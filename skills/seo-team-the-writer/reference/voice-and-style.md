## Default Voice

Direct, conversational, specific, opinionated.

**Prefer:**

- Contractions (don't, won't, can't)
- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)
- Specific numbers ("47 brands" not "many brands")
- Stories over theory when possible
- Active voice over passive

**Brand Context Override:** If `workspace/brand/voice-profile.md` exists, load
and apply it instead of defaults. Note how brand context shaped the content.

## Banned Words and Phrases

### AI-isms (Never Use)

delve, landscape, tapestry, myriad, nuanced, seamless, "in today's fast-paced
world," "it's worth noting," "let's dive in," "in conclusion," "without
further ado," "game-changer," "revolutionize," "paradigm shift," "synergy,"
"unlock the power of," "at the end of the day," "when it comes to"

### Corporate Speak (Never Use)

leverage, harness, cutting-edge, innovative, revolutionary, robust, streamline,
comprehensive, utilize, spearhead, facilitate, optimize (as buzzword — fine
when discussing actual SEO optimization), empower, holistic, scalable,
best-in-class, mission-critical, turnkey, end-to-end, deep dive (as noun)

### Hedge Words (Avoid Unless Genuinely Uncertain)

might, perhaps, arguably, it could be said, possibly, potentially, it's
important to note, it should be mentioned, one might argue, it goes without
saying

## Writing Quality Principles

### Specificity Over Vagueness

- Bad: "Many companies have seen results."
- Good: "23 companies in our study saw first-page rankings within 90 days."

### Original Insights Where Possible

Don't just rephrase what the top 10 already say. Flag where the user should
add personal experience, proprietary data, or contrarian takes.

Use `[EXPERIENCE ANCHOR: Add your story/result about X here]` placeholders
if no brand context exists.

### Anti-AI-Overview Strategy

At each major section, ask: "Could Google's AI Overview answer this section
from publicly available sources?" If yes, the section needs:

- Deeper specificity
- Personal experience or proprietary data
- Contrarian take with specific reasoning
- Interactive elements, tools, or templates

### BLUF (Bottom Line Up Front)

Answer the core query in the first paragraph. Then expand with detail. Readers
(and AI systems) should get the answer immediately, not after 500 words of
context.

## Content Type Characteristics

| Content Type | When to Use | Key Characteristics |
|---|---|---|
| Blog posts / articles | Informational intent | Educational, depth-focused, 1500-3000 words |
| Comparison / "vs" pages | Commercial investigation | Tables, pros/cons, clear winner or recommendation |
| How-to guides | Informational, process | Numbered steps, visuals recommended |
| Pillar pages | Topic cluster hubs | Broad, comprehensive, 3000-5000+ words, links to cluster pages |
| Product/service pages | Transactional | Features, benefits, social proof, CTAs |
| Landing pages | Transactional | Focused CTA, minimal navigation, persuasion-focused |
| Glossary / definition pages | Informational, GEO-friendly | "X is..." format, concise, directly citable |
| FAQ pages | Informational, schema-rich | Q&A pairs, FAQ schema, one paragraph per answer |

## Psychology Frameworks (Conversion Content)

Use when writing product pages, landing pages, or commercial comparison content.

### PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solution)

State the problem. Twist the knife (agitate the pain). Present your solution.

### AIDA (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action)

Hook the reader. Build interest with specifics. Create desire with benefits
and proof. Prompt action with a clear CTA.

### Before-After-Bridge

Show current pain. Paint the desired outcome. Bridge with your
content/product.

## Voice Quality Checklist

Run this before marking content complete:

- [ ] No AI-isms (delve, comprehensive, landscape, seamless, etc.)
- [ ] No corporate speak (leverage, harness, innovative, robust, etc.)
- [ ] No hedge words (might, perhaps, arguably, potentially)
- [ ] Contractions used throughout
- [ ] Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)
- [ ] Specific numbers replace vague quantities
- [ ] Reads naturally when spoken aloud
- [ ] No "as mentioned above" cross-references (self-contained sections)
- [ ] Experience anchors placed where original data/stories should go
